### Indian Trading Market Challenges & Regulatory Constraints

- Developed native algo trading system over 7-8 months testing

- Product ready internally but not user-ready yet
- Tested on own portfolio (₹15 lakhs invested)
- Regulatory barriers in India severely limit scalability:

- Copy trading illegal without proper certifications
- Can’t accept user deposits in hedge fund model
- Can’t sell tips without SEBI registration
- Need NISM certifications, capital requirements, physical office for RA license
- Historical context: 2021 crackdown on 10-15 social investing platforms

- SEBI penalized multiple players
- Influencers fled back to Telegram for user privacy
- Platforms pivoted to educational content

### Crypto Derivatives Platform Strategy

- Building perpetual options on decentralized exchanges

- Solves traditional options problems: monthly expiry, continuous rollover needs
- Enables “perpetual futures without liquidations or losses”
- Custom strike prices (down to 3rd decimal) vs fixed ₹50 intervals
- Technical differentiation from competitors:

- Only 2 companies globally building this solution
- Requires advanced math + finance + blockchain depth
- Built on Uniswap AMM infrastructure as liquidity layer
- Platform advantages over centralized exchanges:

- No custody risk (user funds remain in own wallets)
- Transparency through blockchain
- Reduced liquidation risk ($20B liquidated Oct 10th industry-wide)

### Business Model & Funding Plans

- Revenue streams: exchange fees + brokerage + custody partner margins

- Currently funding through MEV trading and personal capital
- 1.5 years development time, ready for launch
- Fundraising target: $2-2.5M at 10% equity dilution

- Targeting international investors (Europe/US/Singapore/Dubai)
- Avoiding Indian market due to regulatory constraints
- Go-to-market strategy:

- Permissionless derivatives for any tokenized asset
- Custom payoff structures and strike prices
- Appeal to retail traders frustrated with liquidations
