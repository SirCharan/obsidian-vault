### Velvet Capital Strategy Discussion

- HyperLiquid spot-perp arbitrage strategy presented

- Simple mechanism: Buy spot HYPE tokens, sell HYPE perps
- Current funding rate ~50%, strategy backtest shows 15-20% APY potential
- Strategy deployed by MEV Capital on Hyperbeat with $40M TVL, 1% management fee
- Risk considerations from Vasily:

- Increasing competition may reduce funding rates
- Bear market could flip funding rates negative
- Mitigation: Buffer system (offer 12% to users, keep 3-5% buffer like Athena)
- Strategy limitations:

- Only viable on HyperLiquid for decentralized execution due to volume requirements
- Can work on Binance but competes directly with Athena (4% APY on BTC)
- Higher yields possible with meme coins vs BTC/ETH

### Alternative Platform Evaluation

- Aster DEX (BNB chain competitor to HyperLiquid) assessed

- Lacks spot markets - would require on-chain token purchases
- Higher trading fees than HyperLiquid
- Low liquidity/volume (~$10M daily vs HyperLiquid’s billions)
- Funding rates lower (11.95% vs 50% on HyperLiquid)
- Binance execution preferred if pursuing traditional CEX approach

- Would differentiate from Athena through higher-yielding tokens
- Could leverage Velvet’s BNB ecosystem connections for TVL growth

### Time Lock Protocol Overview

- Oracle-less, liquidation-free leverage trading platform

- Novel mechanism using Uniswap V3 LP liquidity
- Fully collateralized positions protect LPs from trader losses
- 1000x leverage possible on any ERC-20 token
- Currently offers 30-40% delta-neutral yield at 50% utilization
- Platform status:

- Demo live on Tenderly fork
- Monad testnet deployment planned in 2 weeks
- 6,000 lines of code, 5 full-time team members
- Waitlist active, frontend launching soon
