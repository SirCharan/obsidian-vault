### Pingu Exchange Overview

- Building perpetual futures platform on Monad testnet
- First perps platform live on Monad (February 21st, 2 days after testnet launch)
- 3 billion volume on Arbitrum in 2024, now focused on V2 for Monad mainnet
- Peer-to-pool model using isolated pools with native assets as collateral
- Buffer mechanism between traders and LPs for stable experience
- Planning community-driven token launch at low FDV rather than raising VC money

### Timelock Trading Mechanism

- Novel tick liquidity borrowing from Uniswap V3 pools
- Traders pay upfront premium (maximum loss amount)
- Can close profitable trades anytime, cannot close losing trades
- LPs remain risk-neutral - only receive impermanent loss they’d get anyway
- Zero slippage trades: 1% of TVL can trade without slippage
- Same mechanism works for any asset with Uniswap liquidity
- Plans for perps product using continuous funding deduction vs upfront premium

### Partnership Opportunities

- Deploy liquidity for popular meme coins

### Growth Strategy & Insights

- Early testnet launch advantage crucial for community building
- Trading competitions and NFT partnerships drove initial traction
- Simple, intuitive UX key to adoption and retention
- Product-centric growth through ease of use and “addictive” trading experience
- Native asset collateral eliminated friction vs other testnet platforms
