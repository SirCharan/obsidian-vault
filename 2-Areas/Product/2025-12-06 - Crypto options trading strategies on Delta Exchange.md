### Trading Strategy Discussion

- Short straddles/strangles on Delta Exchange not profitable due to high fees
- Zero alpha strategies require pricing/execution edge to be viable
- Individual stocks underexplored with profitable opportunities available

- Currently trading there personally with good results
- Delta pricing structure problematic for neutral strategies

- Options heavily priced assuming single-sided directional bets
- High percentage of notional cost structure

### Platform Recommendations

- Delta Exchange limitations for options trading

- Better for futures but not optimal for sophisticated strategies
- Order book liquidity issues during sharp movements
- Superior alternatives for options:

- STS Digital (OTC) - institutional level, requires company KYC
- Paradigm (OTC) - selective onboarding, high minimum trade sizes
- Derivative - deeper liquidity than Delta, better total cost including slippage
- Futures trading alternatives:

- Binance, HyperLiquid (no KYC), Lighter.xyz (zero fees)
- All offer better spreads than Delta

### Strategy Framework Recommendations

- Market phase approach instead of single strategy year-round

- Sideways markets: short strangles or single-sided short calls/puts
- Directional moves: futures with stop loss or ratio call spreads
- Prefer strangles over straddles (less frequent rebalancing)
- 30-45 day expiries optimal for crypto options

- Reduces liquidation risk and allows rebalancing time
- Use maximum 100x leverage advantage on far OTM options

- Example: 70K put for $200 premium on $900 margin = 20% return potential

### Risk Management Insights

- Black swan events affect all exchanges equally

- October 10th example: all order books went dry
- No exchange immune to extreme volatility periods
- Stop loss approach: exit and move to next expiry rather than delta hedging
- Far OTM options benefit from Delta’s market-making obligations

- Higher IV on distant strikes provides “free lunch” opportunity
